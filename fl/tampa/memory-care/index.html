Memory care cost in Tampa, FL (2025): the honest median is $5,950/month

If you're pricing memory care in Tampa this year, the honest median is $5,950 per month — about $1,150 above standard assisted living in the same metro. Florida has the highest concentration of Alzheimer's residents per capita in the country, which means demand pressure shows up in pricing and waitlists more here than in most states. Below: three published sources, what changes the number by sub-area, and how SMMC LTC changes the math for memory care.

Tampa memory care, all-in median (secured 1-bedroom, moderate behavioral needs): roughly $6,400–$7,100/month once you add the behavioral-care upgrade and any separately billed medication management. Premium South Tampa secured units with advanced behavioral care often clear $7,800/month.

Tampa sub-area pricing — what changes by neighborhood

Within the Tampa Bay metro, base monthly memory care varies by roughly $2,250/month from the lowest sub-area to the highest. Here's where the median shifts:

South Tampa, Hyde Park, and Davis Islands

Premium properties, smaller resident-to-staff ratios, higher amenity load. Base monthly typically $7,000–$7,800. Several luxury-tier secured units clear $8,200 with advanced behavioral care.

Westshore, Carrollwood, and Cheval

Mid-premium. Base monthly typically $6,000–$6,800. Newer hospital-system-affiliated MC units (BayCare, AdventHealth) drive this band.

St. Petersburg and Clearwater (Pinellas County)

Strong inventory; mid-tier and value-tier mix. Base monthly typically $5,500–$6,000. More older buildings with renovated secured wings.

Brandon, Riverview, and east Hillsborough

Value-tier MC. Base monthly typically $5,200–$5,600. Older inventory, smaller secured units, often the best value when behavioral needs are moderate.

New Tampa and Pasco County (outer suburbs)

Lowest sub-area median. Base monthly typically $5,000–$5,300. Newer mid-tier MC facilities, longer drive from central Tampa, less established dementia-care infrastructure.

What pushes your Tampa memory care bill above the median

Memory care add-ons differ from standard AL because the secured baseline already includes a lot of what AL bills as "extra." But the bill still moves:

  • Behavioral care upgrade: $400–$1,000/month. Most relevant when a resident has specific behaviors (wandering at night, sundowning, resistance to care, exit-seeking) that require additional one-on-one support.
  • Medication management: $200–$400/month if billed separately. Often bundled into MC base in Tampa — confirm in writing.
  • Incontinence supplies and care: $100–$300/month. More commonly bundled into the MC base than in standard AL — but confirm whether supplies themselves are billed at retail.
  • Hospice overlay (when needed): Typically $0 additional from the facility (hospice is Medicare-billed) but the room continues to bill at the MC rate.
  • Community fee (one-time): $1,500–$5,000 at move-in. Rarely refundable. Tampa MC community fees often run higher than AL because of the dementia-specific intake assessment.
  • Annual rent increase: 3–7% per year on the base. Behavioral-care fees can also rise mid-year if needs change.

Realistic median + likely behavioral add-ons for a moderate-care Tampa MC resident: $6,400–$7,100/month all-in. Heavy-care residents in South Tampa or Westshore often clear $7,800.

Florida SMMC LTC Medicaid: who qualifies and how it works

SMMC LTC covers memory care in Florida, and for many Tampa families this is the single most important fact on this page. SMMC LTC (Statewide Medicaid Managed Care — Long-Term Care) is delivered through assigned managed-care plans (Humana, Sunshine Health, Aetna, Molina, and others) that contract with participating MC facilities. We're not a Medicaid-planning service. But not flagging the timing question would be dishonest.

Why SMMC LTC matters more in memory care than in AL:

  • Memory care stays are typically longer and monthlies are higher, so personal assets are drawn down faster than in standard AL.
  • SMMC LTC planning done early — ideally before assets are exhausted — opens more facility options. Done late, choices narrow.
  • Not every Tampa MC community participates, and the ones that do can have a limited number of plan-funded beds, especially in South Tampa and Westshore.

Florida-specific note that matters: Florida has the highest Alzheimer's population per capita in the United States — over 580,000 Floridians 65+ are living with Alzheimer's, and the share is growing faster than the national rate. That demand pressure shows up two ways: SMMC LTC participation is tighter at the most desirable Tampa facilities, and waitlists for plan-funded MC beds in Tampa Bay run longer than in lower-density states.

Financial eligibility (2025): countable assets at or below $2,000 for a single applicant; monthly income at or below roughly $2,901 (a Qualified Income Trust / Miller Trust handles over-income applicants); the home is generally exempt as long as the applicant or spouse intends to return.

Medical eligibility: the applicant must require a "nursing facility level of care" as defined by Florida's CARES assessment.

If memory care is a likely 2–4 year commitment for your family, get a CARES pre-screening done in the first 90 days of the diagnosis, regardless of current financial position. The math changes 12–24 months in. Talk to an elder law attorney about Florida's look-back rules before you spend down.

Six questions to ask when you tour a Tampa memory care community

  1. What's your daytime caregiver-to-resident ratio in the memory care unit? This is the cost driver — and the answer tells you what you're actually buying. Anything over 1:8 deserves follow-up questions.
  2. What's your night ratio in the memory care wing? Day ratios matter, but night ratios are where most quiet incidents happen — sundowning, wandering, falls.
  3. How is the behavioral-care fee assessed and how often is it reassessed? Ask for the points sheet. Ask what triggers a mid-year increase.
  4. What's included in MC base versus what's an add-on? Walk through the worksheet above line-by-line.
  5. Do you participate in SMMC LTC, and which managed-care plans are you contracted with? Some Tampa MC communities have only a small SMMC LTC contract. Plan-network matters even before eligibility.
  6. What's the current waitlist for plan-funded memory care beds in your community? Florida's Alzheimer's prevalence drives Tampa MC waitlists longer than most states. Confirm specifics — "6 to 18 months" is a common Tampa-area answer.

If a community won't itemize the bill, won't show you the rate increase history, won't disclose the night caregiver ratio, or won't quote a current SMMC LTC waitlist, that's a signal worth weighing.
