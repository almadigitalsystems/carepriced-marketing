Tampa sub-area pricing — what changes by neighborhood

Tampa Bay spans Hillsborough, Pinellas, and Pasco counties. Within the metro, base monthly varies by roughly $1,600/month from the lowest sub-area to the highest. Here's where the median shifts:

South Tampa, Hyde Park, and Davis Islands

Premium real estate, larger units, amenity-driven properties. Base monthly typically $5,400–$6,200. Several luxury-tier communities clear $6,800 with care fees layered in.

Westshore, Carrollwood, and Cheval

Mid-premium. Base monthly typically $4,800–$5,400. Newer inventory and several hospital-system-affiliated communities (BayCare, AdventHealth) drive this band.

St. Petersburg and Clearwater (Pinellas County)

Strong mid-tier inventory. Base monthly typically $4,400–$4,900. Mix of older properties and renovated 1990s-2000s buildings.

Brandon, Riverview, and east Hillsborough

Value-tier suburbs. Base monthly typically $4,200–$4,600. More older inventory, fewer luxury amenities, often the best value for moderate care needs.

New Tampa and Pasco County (outer suburbs)

Lowest sub-area median. Base monthly typically $4,000–$4,400. Newer mid-tier facilities, longer drive from central Tampa, less established care infrastructure.

What pushes your Tampa assisted living bill above the median

The $4,800 base is just the rent + meals + basic services line. The real bill is the base plus add-ons. The add-ons most Tampa families don't see until move-in:

  • Care-level fee: $400–$1,200/month, assessed at move-in based on a points system (mobility, bathing, dressing, eating, toileting, medication). Most Tampa communities reassess every 90 days.
  • Medication management: $200–$400/month if staff administers daily meds. Rarely included in base.
  • Incontinence supplies and care: $100–$300/month. Some communities charge per-incident; others charge a flat add-on.
  • Non-scheduled transportation: $200+/month. Especially relevant in Tampa Bay because medical-specialist trips often span Hillsborough and Pinellas counties — facilities only cover scheduled local transport.
  • Community fee (one-time): $1,500–$5,000 at move-in. Rarely refundable.
  • Annual rent increase: 3–7% per year on the base. Care-level fees can also rise mid-year if needs change.

Realistic median + likely add-ons for a moderate-care Tampa resident: $5,400–$6,000/month all-in. Heavy-care residents in South Tampa or Westshore often clear $6,800.

Florida SMMC LTC Medicaid: who qualifies and how it works

If private-pay isn't sustainable, Florida's Medicaid long-term care program is the SMMC LTC (Statewide Medicaid Managed Care — Long-Term Care) program. SMMC LTC is a managed-care program — meaning if your parent qualifies, the state assigns a managed-care plan (Humana, Sunshine Health, Aetna, Molina, and others) which then arranges care through participating assisted living facilities. Not mentioning SMMC LTC would be dishonest — for many Tampa families, it's the single biggest lever on what you actually pay.

Financial eligibility (2025):

  • Countable assets: at or below $2,000 for a single applicant
  • Monthly income: at or below roughly $2,901 (300% of SSI federal benefit rate); a Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) is the standard Florida workaround for over-income applicants
  • A spouse remaining in the community has separate (more generous) Community Spouse Resource Allowance protections

Medical eligibility: the applicant must require a "nursing facility level of care" as defined by Florida's CARES (Comprehensive Assessment and Review for Long-Term Care Services) assessment.

What SMMC LTC pays: the assigned managed-care plan contracts with participating ALFs and pays the facility directly. The resident keeps a small personal needs allowance; the rest of their income goes toward care.

Many Tampa families don't learn about SMMC LTC until well into their search. Florida's CARES assessment plus financial determination commonly runs 60–90 days, and not every Tampa-area facility participates in SMMC LTC — among those that do, plan-funded beds are limited. We're not a Medicaid-planning service. But if there's any chance you'll need SMMC LTC within 5 years, talk to an elder law attorney about the look-back rules before you spend down.

Six questions to ask when you tour a Tampa assisted living community

  1. What's the last 3 years of base rent increases? If they hesitate, that's a signal worth weighing.
  2. How is the care-level fee assessed and how often is it reassessed? Ask for the points sheet. Ask what triggers a mid-year increase.
  3. What's included in base versus what's an add-on? Walk through the worksheet above line-by-line.
  4. Do you provide non-scheduled transportation across the bay (Pinellas specialists)? Tampa Bay families often drive across counties for specialist appointments. Confirm whether the facility covers it or charges per-trip.
  5. Do you participate in SMMC LTC, and if so, which managed-care plans are you contracted with? Some Tampa communities have only a small SMMC LTC contract — and the answer tells you something about future flexibility if private-pay runs short.
  6. What's your 30-day notice and refund policy if we move out or pass away? Some communities pro-rate; some don't.

If a community won't itemize the bill or won't answer the SMMC LTC question clearly, that's a signal worth weighing.
