How Dallas Senior Care Costs Compare to the National Average

The DFW metro offers one of the more cost-competitive senior care markets among large US cities.

Lower land costs outside North Dallas and Park Cities. The expensive parts of Dallas for senior living are a small geographic slice: Highland Park, University Park, North Dallas, and parts of Plano and Frisco. Communities in Garland, Mesquite, Arlington, Irving, and the southern suburbs run meaningfully cheaper for comparable care.

Texas home care wages are below coastal averages. In-home care in Texas has historically run below the national average on an hourly basis. This gap has narrowed since 2022 but has not closed.

Competitive market with high inventory. DFW has added significant senior living inventory over the past decade. More supply means more price competition, particularly in the mid-tier assisted living market. Communities in the $3,200–$4,000/month range compete actively for residents.

What's Driving 2026 Cost Increases in Dallas

Staffing competition in the I-35 corridor. The DFW labor market is one of the tightest in Texas. Direct-care workers have more employment options than in smaller Texas markets, and facilities that want to maintain staffing ratios are paying for it.

Premium corridor demand. North Dallas, Allen, Frisco, McKinney, and Collin County broadly are experiencing senior care demand growing faster than new supply. Communities in these corridors are holding prices firm.

National trend overlay. The 4.4% YoY national increase in assisted living reflects real cost-of-operations increases — food, utilities, insurance, labor — that are not Texas-specific. DFW communities are absorbing the same input cost increases as their national peers.

How a Dallas Family's Budget Actually Comes Together

Here's how a real Dallas family's number actually adds up. This is a worked example, not a quote.

A family in Plano looking at assisted living for a parent who needs Level 2 care (help with 2 ADLs, medication management, structured daily routine) might see:

  • Base rate (studio, Level 1): ~$3,900/month
  • Care-level upcharge (Level 2 of 4): +$450/month
  • Medication management: +$250/month
  • Incontinence care if applicable: +$175/month
  • Community/move-in fee (one-time, amortized): +$175/month equivalent

Realistic 12-month average: ~$4,950/month

The brochure said "from $3,900." The realistic number is higher. The gap between them is what Carepriced helps you anticipate.

Texas Medicaid and the STAR+PLUS Waiver for Dallas Families

  • STAR+PLUS covers some home and community-based services (personal attendant care, adult day care) but Texas Medicaid does NOT broadly cover assisted living facility costs. This is a critical planning distinction.
  • Nursing home care is covered by Texas Medicaid for residents who meet clinical and financial eligibility.
  • Texas does not have a robust Medicaid pathway to assisted living. Families who need assisted living and are on Medicaid have fewer options than families in states like Arizona (ALTCS) or Florida (SMMC-LTC). Plan accordingly.
  • Veterans' Aid & Attendance provides $2,200+/month toward qualifying veterans' or surviving spouses' care costs. DFW has a large veteran population — many families never apply.
  • Long-term care insurance. Texas-based families who purchased LTCi policies 10–15 years ago may have benefits covering $3,000–$5,000/month toward assisted living. Many families don't know what benefit period they purchased or whether inflation protection was included.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average cost of assisted living in Dallas?

Nationally, assisted living averages $4,419/month in 2026. DFW-area assisted living typically runs $3,200–$4,500/month in most suburban corridors, with North Dallas and Plano communities at $4,500–$5,500+/month.

Is memory care in Dallas more expensive than regular assisted living?

Yes — significantly. Memory care in DFW typically runs $5,500–$7,500/month, versus $3,200–$5,500/month for assisted living. The higher cost reflects dedicated dementia training, secured units, and higher staffing ratios.

Does Texas Medicaid cover assisted living in Dallas?

No — not broadly. Texas Medicaid (STAR+PLUS) covers some home and community-based services but not assisted living facility costs. Nursing home Medicaid is available for those who qualify.

Is home care in Dallas cheaper than assisted living?

At lower hours, yes. In-home care in DFW runs roughly $22–$30/hour depending on agency and care type — below the national average of $30/hr. At 40+ hours/week, the comparison shifts. Carepriced's calculator lets you run this at your specific hours.
