Chicago sub-area pricing — what changes by neighborhood

Within the Chicago metro, base monthly varies by roughly $2,300/month from the lowest sub-area to the highest. Here's where the median shifts:

North Shore (Evanston, Wilmette)

Higher land cost, lakefront proximity, newer construction. Base monthly typically $6,200–$6,800. Several premium communities clear $8,500 with care fees.

Lincoln Park and Gold Coast

Urban core, walkable, high-amenity. Base monthly typically $5,800–$6,200. Smaller floor plans, higher per-square-foot cost, closest to downtown family neighborhoods.

Oak Park and Western Suburbs

Mid-tier. Base monthly typically $4,800–$5,200. Mix of established buildings and recent renovations. Good transit access to downtown and strong value for moderate care needs.

South Suburbs (Orland Park, Tinley Park)

Below metro median. Base monthly typically $4,000–$4,500. Larger campuses, more independent-living-to-AL transitions, often the best value for families south of the city.

Northwest Suburbs (Arlington Heights, Schaumburg)

Slightly below metro median. Base monthly typically $4,400–$4,800. Suburban campuses, good highway access, strong mix of mid-tier communities. If proximity to the lakefront isn't a priority, this is where the dollars stretch.

What pushes your Chicago assisted living bill above the median

The $5,400 base is just the rent + meals + basic services line. The real bill is the base plus add-ons. The add-ons most families don't see until move-in:

  • Care-level fee: $400–$1,400/month, assessed at move-in based on a points system (mobility, bathing, dressing, eating, toileting, medication). Most Chicago communities reassess every 90 days.
  • Medication management: $200–$400/month if staff administers daily meds. This is rarely included in base.
  • Incontinence supplies and care: $100–$300/month. Some communities charge per-incident; others charge a flat add-on.
  • Two-person transfer: $300–$600/month if your family member can't transfer independently.
  • Community fee (one-time): $1,500–$5,000 at move-in. Rarely refundable.
  • Annual rent increase: 3–7% per year on the base. Care-level fees can also rise mid-year if needs change.

Realistic median + likely add-ons for a moderate-care Chicago resident: $6,200–$6,800/month all-in. Heavy-care residents clear $8,000.

Illinois Supportive Living Program (SLP): who qualifies and how it works

If private-pay isn't sustainable, Illinois's Medicaid assisted living program is called the Supportive Living Program (SLP). Not mentioning SLP would be dishonest — for many Chicago families, it's the difference between staying in a community and leaving it.

Financial eligibility (2025):

  • Countable assets: at or below $2,000 for a single applicant
  • Monthly income: at or below roughly $2,829
  • The home is generally exempt as long as the applicant or spouse intends to return

Medical eligibility: the applicant must require a "nursing home level of care" as assessed by a Determination of Need (DON) screening.

What SLP pays: SLP contracts with specific supportive living facilities and pays them directly. The resident keeps a small personal needs allowance; the rest of their income goes toward care.

Many families don't learn about SLP until well into their search. If there's any chance you'll need it within 5 years, talk to an elder law attorney about asset planning before you spend down. More information: Illinois Department of Healthcare and Family Services.

Six questions to ask when you tour a Chicago assisted living community

  1. What's the last 3 years of base rent increases? If they hesitate, that's a signal worth weighing.
  2. How is the care-level fee assessed and how often is it reassessed? Ask for the points sheet. Ask what triggers a mid-year increase.
  3. What's included in base versus what's an add-on? Walk through the worksheet above line-by-line.
  4. What's your staff-to-resident ratio at night? Day ratios matter, but night ratios are where most quiet incidents happen.
  5. Do you accept SLP Medicaid, and if a current private-pay resident transitions to SLP, can they stay? Some communities have only a small SLP contract — and may move residents to a different building when they convert.
  6. What's your 30-day notice and refund policy if we move out or pass away? Some communities pro-rate; some don't.

If a community won't itemize the bill or won't show you the rate increase history, that's a signal worth weighing.
