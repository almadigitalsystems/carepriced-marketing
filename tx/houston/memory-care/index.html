Memory care cost in Houston, TX (2025): the honest median is $6,350/month

If you're pricing memory care in Houston this year, the honest median is $6,350 per month — about $1,150 above standard assisted living in the same metro. Not a range. Not "starting at." A real-world median, before behavioral-care add-ons. Texas operates STAR+PLUS as managed-care with multiple participating health plans, so plan-network matters as much as facility selection. Below: three published sources, what changes the number by sub-area across Greater Houston, and how STAR+PLUS changes the math for memory care.

Houston sub-area pricing — what changes by neighborhood

Within Greater Houston, base monthly memory care varies by roughly $3,100/month from the lowest sub-area to the highest — the same swing we see in standard AL, for the same reasons.

River Oaks, Memorial, and Tanglewood

Premium MC; lower caregiver ratios; high amenity load. Base monthly typically $7,800–$8,400. Several luxury-tier secured units clear $8,500 with advanced behavioral care.

Galleria, Bellaire, and West University

Inner Loop, Heights, and Montrose

Sugar Land, Katy, and The Woodlands

Newer master-planned community MC; mid-tier. Base monthly typically $5,900–$6,400. Often the best value for moderate behavioral needs.

What pushes your Houston memory care bill above the median

Memory care add-ons differ from standard AL because the secured baseline already includes a lot of what AL bills as "extra." But the bill still moves:

  • Behavioral care upgrade: $400–$1,000/month. Most relevant when a resident has specific behaviors (wandering at night, sundowning, resistance to care, exit-seeking) that require additional one-on-one support.
  • Medication management: $200–$400/month if billed separately. Often bundled into MC base in Houston — confirm in writing.
  • Incontinence supplies and care: $100–$300/month. More commonly bundled into the MC base than in standard AL — but confirm whether supplies themselves are billed at retail.
  • Hospice overlay (when needed): Typically $0 additional from the facility (hospice is Medicare-billed) but the room continues to bill at the MC rate.
  • Community fee (one-time): $1,500–$5,000 at move-in. Rarely refundable. Houston MC community fees often run on the higher end because of the dementia-specific intake assessment.
  • Annual rent increase: 3–7% per year on the base. Behavioral-care fees can also rise mid-year if needs change.

Realistic median + likely behavioral add-ons for a moderate-care Houston MC resident: $6,800–$7,500/month all-in. Heavy-care residents in Memorial or River Oaks often clear $8,500.

Texas STAR+PLUS Medicaid: who qualifies and how it works

STAR+PLUS covers memory care in Texas, and for many Houston families this is the single most important fact on this page. STAR+PLUS is Texas's managed-care program for Medicaid long-term services and supports — the same program that covers assisted living. We're not a Medicaid-planning service. But not flagging the timing question would be dishonest.

Why STAR+PLUS matters more in memory care than in AL:

  • Memory care stays are typically longer and monthlies are higher, so personal assets are drawn down faster than in standard AL.
  • STAR+PLUS planning done early — ideally before assets are exhausted — opens more facility options. Done late, choices narrow.
  • Not every Houston MC community participates, and the ones that do can have a limited number of plan-funded beds, especially in Memorial, Galleria, and West University.

Texas-specific note that matters: Texas operates STAR+PLUS as managed-care with multiple participating health plans (Amerigroup, Molina, Superior, United, etc.), and which plan you're enrolled in affects which Houston facilities are in-network. Switching plans is allowed at limited points in the year, so if the right MC facility is only in-network for one plan, that's a planning conversation worth having with a benefits counselor early.

Financial eligibility (2025): countable assets at or below $2,000 for a single applicant; monthly income at or below approximately $2,901 (a Miller Trust is required for many over-income applicants — not optional in Texas); the home is generally exempt with intent-to-return; Texas operates Medicaid Estate Recovery so plan accordingly.

Medical eligibility: the applicant must require a "nursing facility level of care" as assessed by Texas HHSC.

If memory care is a likely 2–4 year commitment for your family, get the Texas HHSC eligibility screening initiated in the first 90 days of the diagnosis, regardless of current financial position. The math changes 12–24 months in. Talk to an elder law attorney about Texas's 5-year look-back rules before you spend down.

Six questions to ask when you tour a Houston memory care community

  1. What's your daytime caregiver-to-resident ratio in the memory care unit? This is the cost driver — and the answer tells you what you're actually buying. Anything over 1:8 deserves follow-up questions.
  2. What's your night ratio in the memory care wing? Day ratios matter, but night ratios are where most quiet incidents happen — sundowning, wandering, falls.
  3. How is the behavioral-care fee assessed and how often is it reassessed? Ask for the points sheet. Ask what triggers a mid-year increase.
  4. What's included in MC base versus what's an add-on? Walk through the worksheet above line-by-line.
  5. Do you participate in STAR+PLUS, and which managed-care plans are you contracted with? Plan-network matters even before eligibility — Texas restricts plan-switching to specific points in the year. The right MC facility may be in-network for only one plan.
  6. What's the current waitlist for plan-funded memory care beds in your community? Memorial, Galleria, and West University MC communities often have STAR+PLUS bed waitlists. Confirm specifics.

If a community won't itemize the bill, won't disclose the night caregiver ratio, or won't answer the STAR+PLUS plan-network question clearly, that's a signal worth weighing.
