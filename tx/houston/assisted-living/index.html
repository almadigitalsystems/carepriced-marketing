Assisted living cost in Houston, TX (2025): the honest median is $5,200/month

If you're pricing assisted living in Houston this year, the honest median is $5,200 per month. Not a range. Not "starting at." A real-world median, before care-level fees and add-ons. Houston tracks above the Texas state line because of its size and the premium inner-loop submarkets — and the metro spans River Oaks to far suburbs, so the sub-area swing is big. Below: three published sources, what changes by neighborhood, and the line-items most families don't see until move-in.

Houston assisted living, all-in median (1-bedroom, moderate care needs): roughly $5,800–$6,400/month once you add the typical care-level fee, medication management, and incontinence supplies. Premium Memorial or River Oaks residents with heavier care needs often clear $7,500/month.

Houston sub-area pricing — what changes by neighborhood

Greater Houston spans Harris, Fort Bend, and Montgomery counties. Within the metro, base monthly varies by roughly $3,100/month from the lowest sub-area to the highest. Here's where the median shifts:

River Oaks, Memorial, and Tanglewood

Premium properties; smaller resident-to-staff ratios; high amenity load. Base monthly typically $6,600–$7,400. Several luxury-tier communities clear $7,500 with care fees layered in.

Galleria, Bellaire, and West University

Newer mid-premium product; medical-center-affiliated communities (Houston Methodist, Memorial Hermann). Base monthly typically $5,700–$6,300.

Inner Loop, Heights, and Montrose

Mix of mid-tier and renovated older inventory. Base monthly typically $5,200–$5,800. Closest to the Texas Medical Center for specialist visits.

Sugar Land, Katy, and The Woodlands

Newer master-planned communities; mid-tier inventory. Base monthly typically $4,800–$5,300. Often the best value for moderate care needs.

Pearland, Friendswood, Clear Lake (south)

Value-tier and mid-tier mix. Base monthly typically $4,400–$4,800. Older inventory, less luxury, longer drive to the Medical Center.

Kingwood, Spring, and far suburbs (Hwy 6, Hwy 249, FM 1960)

Lowest sub-area median. Base monthly typically $3,800–$4,400. Basic studios, longer drive from medical centers, smaller staffing models.

What pushes your Houston assisted living bill above the median

The $5,200 base is just the rent + meals + basic services line. The real bill is the base plus add-ons. The add-ons most Houston families don't see until move-in:

  • Care-level fee: $400–$1,500/month, depending on how the facility scores activities of daily living (ADLs). Most Houston communities have 3–5 care levels and reassess every 90 days.
  • Medication management: $200–$400/month. Almost always billed separately from base rent.
  • Incontinence supplies and care: $100–$300/month. Confirm whether the supplies themselves are included or billed at retail.
  • Non-scheduled transportation: $150–$300/month. Scheduled medical transport is often included; on-demand specialty trips to the Medical Center are typically extra.
  • Community fee (one-time): $1,500–$5,000 at move-in. Rarely refundable.
  • Annual rent increase: 3–7% per year on the base. Care-level fees can also rise mid-year if needs change.

Realistic median + likely add-ons for a moderate-care Houston resident: $5,800–$6,400/month all-in. Heavy-care residents in Memorial, River Oaks, or Galleria often clear $7,500.

Texas STAR+PLUS Medicaid: who qualifies and how it works

If your parent's assets are likely to draw down within 2–4 years, the most consequential thing on this page is Texas's Medicaid long-term-care program: STAR+PLUS. STAR+PLUS is the Texas managed-care program that delivers Medicaid long-term services and supports for elderly and disabled adults. We're not a Medicaid-planning service. But not mentioning STAR+PLUS would be dishonest — for many Houston families, it's the difference between staying in a community and leaving it.

How STAR+PLUS works: When a participant qualifies, the assigned managed-care plan (Amerigroup, Molina, Superior, United, etc.) contracts with participating assisted living facilities. Which plan you're enrolled in affects which Houston facilities are in-network. Switching plans is allowed only at limited points in the year, so plan-network is a planning conversation worth having early.

Two things many Texas families don't know early enough:

  • Many families don't learn about STAR+PLUS until well into their search, after substantial private-pay months have already eaten into the assets that would have funded a longer stay.
  • Not every Houston-area assisted living facility participates. Among the ones that do, plan-funded beds are limited and waitlist times vary by managed-care plan and facility.

Financial eligibility (2025):

  • Countable assets: at or below $2,000 for a single applicant
  • Monthly income: at or below approximately $2,901 (300% of SSI federal benefit rate); a Qualified Income Trust (Miller Trust) is the standard Texas workaround for over-income applicants — required for many AL applicants, not optional
  • The home is generally exempt as long as the applicant or spouse intends to return; Texas does operate Medicaid Estate Recovery, so plan accordingly

Medical eligibility: the applicant must require a "nursing facility level of care" as assessed by Texas HHSC.

If AL is going to be a 2+ year commitment for your family, get the Texas HHSC eligibility screening initiated in the first 90 days of the search, regardless of current financial position. Talk to an elder law attorney about the 5-year look-back on asset transfers before you spend down.

Six questions to ask when you tour a Houston assisted living community

  1. What's the last 3 years of base rent increases? If they hesitate, that's a signal worth weighing.
  2. How is the care-level fee assessed and how often is it reassessed? Ask for the points sheet. Ask what triggers a mid-year increase.
  3. What's included in base versus what's an add-on? Walk through the worksheet above line-by-line.
  4. Do you provide transportation to Texas Medical Center for specialist visits? Specialist trips to TMC are common in Houston and can be billed per-trip if they're not in the scheduled-transport bucket.
  5. Do you participate in STAR+PLUS, and which managed-care plans are you contracted with? Plan-network matters even before eligibility — switching plans is restricted to specific points in the year, so the right facility may be in-network for only one plan.
  6. What's your 30-day notice and refund policy if we move out or pass away? Some communities pro-rate; some don't.

If a community won't itemize the bill, won't show you the rate increase history, or won't answer the STAR+PLUS plan question clearly, that's a signal worth weighing.
