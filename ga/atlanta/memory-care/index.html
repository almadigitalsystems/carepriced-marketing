Atlanta sub-area pricing — what changes by neighborhood

Within metro Atlanta, base monthly memory care varies by roughly $2,100/month from the lowest sub-area to the highest.

Buckhead, Sandy Springs, and Dunwoody

Premium MC; lower caregiver ratios; high amenity load. Base monthly typically $7,200–$7,800. Several luxury-tier secured units clear $7,800 with advanced behavioral care.

Midtown, Atlantic Station, and Brookhaven

Mid-premium MC. Base monthly typically $6,300–$6,800. Newer hospital-system-affiliated MC units (Emory Healthcare, Piedmont) drive this band.

Decatur, North Druid Hills, and East Atlanta

Mid-tier MC. Base monthly typically $6,000–$6,500. Mix of newer and renovated secured wings.

Marietta, Kennesaw, and Roswell (north Cobb)

Slightly below metro median. Base monthly typically $5,700–$6,100. Newer master-planned community MC; mid-tier inventory.

Lawrenceville, Snellville, and Suwanee (Gwinnett)

Value-tier and mid-tier MC mix. Base monthly typically $5,500–$5,900. Older inner-suburb inventory.

Stockbridge, McDonough, and south metro suburbs

Lowest sub-area median. Base monthly typically $5,300–$5,700. Smaller secured units, longer drive from the Perimeter.

What pushes your Atlanta memory care bill above the median

Memory care add-ons differ from standard AL because the secured baseline already includes a lot of what AL bills as "extra." But the bill still moves:

  • Behavioral care upgrade: $400–$1,000/month. Most relevant when a resident has specific behaviors (wandering at night, sundowning, resistance to care, exit-seeking) that require additional one-on-one support.
  • Medication management: $200–$400/month if billed separately. Often bundled into MC base in Atlanta — confirm in writing.
  • Incontinence supplies and care: $100–$300/month. More commonly bundled in MC than in AL — but confirm whether supplies themselves are billed at retail.
  • Hospice overlay (when needed): Typically $0 additional from the facility (hospice is Medicare-billed) but the room continues to bill at the MC rate.
  • Community fee (one-time): $1,500–$5,000 at move-in. Rarely refundable. Atlanta MC community fees often run on the higher end because of the dementia-specific intake assessment.
  • Annual rent increase: 3–7% per year on the base. Behavioral-care fees can also rise mid-year if needs change.

Realistic median + likely behavioral add-ons for a moderate-care Atlanta MC resident: $6,700–$7,400/month all-in. Heavy-care residents in Buckhead or Sandy Springs often clear $7,800.

Georgia CCSP and SOURCE Medicaid: who qualifies and how it works

CCSP and SOURCE cover memory care in Georgia, and for many Atlanta families this is the single most important fact on this page. We're not a Medicaid-planning service. But not flagging the timing question would be dishonest.

Why these waivers matter more in memory care than in AL:

  • Memory care stays are typically longer and monthlies are higher, so personal assets are drawn down faster than in standard AL.
  • Both CCSP and SOURCE have meaningful waitlists in Georgia. Planning done early — ideally before assets are exhausted — opens more facility options. Done late, choices narrow.
  • Not every Atlanta MC community participates, and the ones that do can have a limited number of plan-funded beds, especially in Buckhead and Sandy Springs.

Georgia-specific note that matters: Georgia's Medicaid LTC waitlists are real — even financially-eligible applicants can wait 3–9 months for a CCSP or SOURCE slot. Apply earlier than you think you need to. The waitlist is the binding constraint, not just the financial determination.

Financial eligibility (2025): countable assets at or below $2,000 for a single applicant; monthly income at or below approximately $2,901 (Miller Trust handles over-income applicants); the home is generally exempt with intent-to-return; Georgia operates Medicaid Estate Recovery.

Medical eligibility: the applicant must require a "nursing facility level of care" as assessed by Georgia DCH.

If memory care is a likely 2–4 year commitment for your family, get the Georgia DCH eligibility screening initiated in the first 90 days of the diagnosis. The math changes 12–24 months in. Talk to an elder law attorney about Georgia's look-back rules before you spend down.

Six questions to ask when you tour an Atlanta memory care community

  1. What's your daytime caregiver-to-resident ratio in the memory care unit? This is the cost driver — and the answer tells you what you're actually buying. Anything over 1:8 deserves follow-up questions.
  2. What's your night ratio in the memory care wing? Day ratios matter, but night ratios are where most quiet incidents happen — sundowning, wandering, falls.
  3. How is the behavioral-care fee assessed and how often is it reassessed? Ask for the points sheet. Ask what triggers a mid-year increase.
  4. What's included in MC base versus what's an add-on? Walk through the worksheet above line-by-line.
  5. Do you participate in CCSP or SOURCE, and what's the current waitlist for plan-funded MC beds? Georgia's Medicaid LTC waitlists are real — confirm specifics. "3 to 9 months" is a common Atlanta-area answer.
  6. What's your 30-day notice and refund policy if we move out or pass away? Some communities pro-rate; some don't. MC residents often have shorter average stays than AL, which makes this question matter more.

If a community won't itemize the bill, won't disclose the night caregiver ratio, or won't quote a current CCSP/SOURCE waitlist, that's a signal worth weighing.
