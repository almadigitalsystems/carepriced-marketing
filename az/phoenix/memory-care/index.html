Phoenix sub-area pricing — what changes by neighborhood

Within the Phoenix metro, base monthly memory care varies by roughly $2,300/month from the lowest sub-area to the highest. Here's where the median shifts:

Scottsdale and North Scottsdale

Premium properties, smaller resident-to-staff ratios, higher amenity load. Base monthly typically $7,400–$8,200. Several luxury-tier secured units clear $8,800 with advanced behavioral care.

Central Phoenix and Arcadia

Mid-tier. Base monthly typically $6,200–$7,000. Mix of established communities with renovated secured wings and recently opened standalone memory care.

Mesa, Tempe, and Chandler (East Valley)

Slightly below metro median. Base monthly typically $5,800–$6,500. Older inventory, more value-tier secured units, often the best value when behavioral needs are moderate.

Glendale, Peoria, Surprise (West Valley)

Lowest sub-area median. Base monthly typically $5,200–$5,900. Smaller communities, fewer luxury amenities. If proximity to Scottsdale isn't a priority and you're managing private-pay carefully, this is where the dollars stretch.

What pushes your Phoenix memory care bill above the median

Memory care add-ons differ from standard AL because the secured baseline already includes a lot of what AL bills as "extra." But the bill still moves:

  • Behavioral care upgrade: $400–$1,000/month, assessed at move-in based on specific behaviors (wandering at night, sundowning, resistance to care, exit-seeking). Most Phoenix communities reassess every 90 days.
  • Medication management: $200–$400/month if billed separately. Often bundled into MC base — confirm in writing.
  • Incontinence supplies and care: $100–$300/month. More commonly bundled into the MC base than in standard AL — but confirm whether supplies themselves are billed at retail.
  • Hospice overlay (when needed): Typically $0 additional from the facility (hospice is Medicare-billed) but the room continues to bill at the MC rate. Coordination details matter.
  • Community fee (one-time): $1,500–$5,000 at move-in. Rarely refundable. MC community fees often run higher than AL because of the assessment intake.
  • Annual rent increase: 3–7% per year on the base. Behavioral-care fees can also rise mid-year if needs change.

Realistic median + likely behavioral add-ons for a moderate-care Phoenix MC resident: $7,000–$7,800/month all-in. Heavy-care or premium-Scottsdale residents clear $8,500.

Arizona ALTCS Medicaid: who qualifies and how it works

If private-pay isn't sustainable, Arizona's Medicaid long-term care program is called ALTCS (Arizona Long Term Care System). ALTCS covers memory care in Arizona, and not mentioning it would be dishonest — for many Phoenix families, it's the difference between staying in a community and leaving it.

Why ALTCS matters more in memory care than in AL:

  • Memory care stays are typically longer and monthlies are higher, so personal assets are drawn down faster than in standard AL.
  • ALTCS planning done early — ideally before assets are exhausted — opens more facility options. Done late, choices narrow.
  • Not every Phoenix MC community participates in ALTCS, and the ones that do can have waitlists, especially in Scottsdale and central Phoenix.

Financial eligibility (2025):

  • Countable assets: at or below $2,000 for a single applicant
  • Monthly income: at or below roughly $2,901 (300% of SSI federal benefit rate)
  • The home is generally exempt as long as the applicant or spouse intends to return

Medical eligibility: the applicant must require a "nursing home level of care" as assessed by an ALTCS Pre-Admission Screening (PAS).

What ALTCS pays: ALTCS contracts with specific memory care facilities and pays them directly. The resident keeps a small personal needs allowance ($138.20/month in 2025); the rest of their income goes toward care.

If memory care is a likely 2–4 year commitment for your family, get an ALTCS pre-screening done in the first 90 days of the diagnosis, regardless of current financial position. Six questions to ask when you tour a Phoenix memory care community

  1. What's your daytime caregiver-to-resident ratio in the memory care unit? This is the cost driver — and the answer tells you what you're actually buying. Anything over 1:8 deserves follow-up questions.
  2. What's your night ratio in the memory care wing? Day ratios matter, but night ratios are where most quiet incidents happen — sundowning, wandering, falls. Worth asking explicitly.
  3. How is the behavioral-care fee assessed and how often is it reassessed? Ask for the points sheet. Ask what triggers a mid-year increase.
  4. What's included in MC base versus what's an add-on? Walk through the worksheet above line-by-line.
  5. Do you accept ALTCS, and if a current private-pay resident transitions to ALTCS, can they stay in the same secured unit? Some communities have only a small ALTCS contract and may move residents to a different building when they convert.
  6. What's your 30-day notice and refund policy if we move out or pass away? Some communities pro-rate; some don't. MC residents often have shorter average stays than AL, which makes this question matter more.

If a community won't itemize the bill, won't show you the rate increase history, or won't disclose the night caregiver ratio, that's a signal worth weighing.
